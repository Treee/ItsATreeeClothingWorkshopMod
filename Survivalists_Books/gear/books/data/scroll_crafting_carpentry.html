In my line of work a tradesmans craft is his secret but with the apocalypse ruining any chance at winning woodworking awards I feel the need to immortalize my craft on paper. Written here are a few recipes for success.


I once made the most beautiful dresser and no one ever got to see it. Such a shame to waste beauty. If I remember correctly, I felled several tall trees and fashioned the straightest planks you ever saw. Once finished, I hammered the boards together and used the extra to make a drawer that slides in. Be careful though, you might fall into the void with all that space.


The mind is a terrible thing to waste especially when the most dangerous game can sit across from you while playing. A chess board can provide the perfect battlefield to prove your mettle against rivals. The finest board can be fashioned from the wood of a large tree. The pieces are carved from bark; the finer the wood the better. Settle your scores on the board instead of with bullets.


Some people say the age of enlightenment was in the early 1700s but I say it is today. The world is so dark but if you can hang lights you can brighten someone's day. The easiest way to make a street lampost is fashion some straight planks from tall trees. Take those planks and nail them together to make an L shaped post. Place your post and brighten the world. If you have leftover planks try making a table. The hardest part is ensuring the legs are the same length.


You are thriving in the world destroyed by the walking dead and need a place to store all your gear but without the risk of anything being stolen. So you need to know how to build walls, doors, and simple barricades. Luckily the basics are quite simple. First find small sticks that can form the frame of what you want. Take rope and bind it together so it holds the shape. You can make many barriers this way to secure your goods.
